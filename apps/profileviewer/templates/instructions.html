Welcome

We are conducting research on finding experts who can give suggestions about where to go. Basically your part in this research is looking into a candidate's profile depicted in a bunch of charts and determining to what extent the candidate knows about the given places.

Four pie charts show what (kinds of) places a candidate has been to and the distribution of frequencies, including top-level categories, lower-level categories, places, and cities.

A bar chart displays a candidate's visiting timeline: the number of the candidate's visiting records to a set of places per week.

A map shows a candidate's foot print around different places.

All the charts are interactive

When you click on a pie slice or drag a segment over the timeline, the other charts will be updated to show the selected range of visits.

A list of recommended filters are provided. Clicking on these buttons will make the charts focus on the selected visits, similar to clicking on a pie slice. DO NOT fully rely on the recommended filters, they may not be sufficient.

Having trouble restoring the states of the charts? Click the reset button.

After investigation, you may have a feeling about how likely is it, from the candidate, to get sufficient information about the given places. Based on this, please assign a score to their expertise on a scale from 1 (Novices) to 5 (Experts).

Pro tip: If you are confused about a chart or a term, hover your mouse over it.
