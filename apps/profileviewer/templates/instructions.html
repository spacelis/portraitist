Welcome

We are conducting a research on finding experts who can give suggestions about where to go. It is great that you come and help us evaluate the candidates.

Basically you will look into a candidate's profile depicted in a bunch of charts. These charts show the distribution of places the candidate has been to. For example, the chart on the left shows that the candidate has been to a lot of places about food.

Besides the distribution over places, we also provide a bar chart displaying a candidate's visiting timeline. It shows the number of times the candidate visited different places per week.

In addition, a map is provided to show a candidate's foot print around different places.

All the charts are interactive:

When you click on a slice of pie charts or drag a segment with the mouse on the timeline, the other charts will be updated to show only the selected range of visits.

To help you quickly get to interesting parts of the candidates' visits, a list of recommended filters are provided. Clicking on these buttons will make the interface focus on the selected visits, similar to clicking on a pie slice. Do not fully rely on the recommended filters, they may not be sufficient for a judgement.

If you have trouble restoring the states of charts, you may always click the reset button.

After your investigation, you may have a feeling about how likely is it for you to get sufficient information about the places from a candidate. Based on this, you assign a score to their expertise on a scale from 1 (Novice) to 5 (Expert). We sincerely appreciate your work!

Pro tip: If you are confused about a chart or a term, try hover your mouse over it.
